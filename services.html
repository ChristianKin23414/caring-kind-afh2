Frequently asked

Questions families often ask

If you don't see your question here, we'd love to chat — call us anytime.

What is an Adult Family Home?
An Adult Family Home is a state-licensed residence that provides room, board, laundry, supervision, and personal care for a small number of adults — typically up to six — in a real home setting.
How many residents do you care for?
We intentionally keep our home small so each resident receives attentive, personalized care. Our small size is one of the things families appreciate most.
Can residents bring their own furniture?
Absolutely. Familiar belongings — a favorite chair, family photos, a special quilt — help residents feel at home, and we encourage it.
Are visitors welcome?
Yes! Family and friends are always welcome. We love seeing residents surrounded by the people they love.
Do you handle medications?
Yes. We carefully manage and administer medications according to each resident's care plan, in coordination with their physicians and pharmacy.
How do I get started?
The best first step is a quiet, no-pressure tour. Call us or use our contact form, and we'll find a time that works for your family.
What types of care needs can you accommodate?
We support a range of needs — from light personal care to more advanced support including mobility, memory care, and end-of-life comfort care. We're happy to discuss your loved one's specific situation.

Wondering if we're the right fit?

We'd be glad to talk through your loved one's care needs and answer your questions — no pressure, no rush.
